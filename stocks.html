BECOME A COMPANY PART-OWNER

A stock or share (also known as "equity") is a financial instrument that expresses ownership of a firm or company and constitutes a proportionate claim on its assets (what it owns) and earnings (what it receives in profits). Stock ownership means the shareholder holds a slice of business equal to the number of shares held as a share of the company's total outstanding shares. For example, a person or company owning the 100,000 shares of a company with 1 million outstanding shares would have a 10 per cent stake in it. Many companies have millions or billions of shares outstanding.

If the thought of investing in the stock market is frightening you are not alone. Individuals with very limited stock investing experience are either frightened by the horror stories of the average investor losing 50 percent of their portfolio value – for instance, in the two bear markets that have already taken place in this millennium – or are seduced by "hot tips" promising enormous rewards but rarely paying off. So it's no surprise that the pendulum of investment sentiment is said to swing between fear and greed.
